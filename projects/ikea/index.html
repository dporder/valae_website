Overview

Making the best better

IKEA's Billie is retail's most ambitious AI chatbot programme — a generative AI-powered assistant that has handled over 3.2 million customer interactions across 63 markets and dozens of languages. It enabled the reskilling of 8,500 call centre workers into higher-value roles as interior design advisors.

Valae was brought in to make Billie smarter. Where most AI projects start from scratch, this one starts from excellence — and the challenge is making it measurably, systematically better. Valae is now embedded within IKEA's global GenAI chatbot team, applying systematic improvements across the entire system.

The Challenge

Scale creates its own problems

Billie is one of retail's most high-profile AI deployments. But at IKEA's scale, complexity compounds fast. Every improvement must work across dozens of markets, languages, and product categories simultaneously.

Prompt complexity at scale
Billie's behaviour is driven by layered prompt systems — system prompts, tool planners, response generators, and judge layers. At global scale, these grow organically and become increasingly hard to improve.
Disconnected quality tools
Scoring systems, validation tools, and monitoring dashboards existed but weren't connected. Without a continuous feedback loop, improvements were based on intuition rather than data.
Classification leaking value
Misclassifying a customer's intent — routing a delivery question into a returns flow — cascades into wrong answers, unnecessary escalations, and contaminated quality data.
Support leaving money on the table
Customers routinely signal buying intent — asking about dimensions, kitchen planning, stock availability. The chatbot treated these as support queries, resolved them, and moved on.
Knowledge at scale
Content spanning 63 markets, dozens of languages, and years of accumulated documentation. Manually maintained, with no way to detect when information goes quietly stale.
Multi-market complexity
63 markets with different languages, regulations, product catalogues, and customer expectations. Changes that improve performance in one market risk degrading another.
The Solution

Six workstreams. One smarter chatbot.

Valae is embedded within IKEA's global GenAI chatbot team, applying the same Step Zero methodology at a fundamentally different scale. Six parallel workstreams, each designed to make Billie measurably better.

Prompt Architecture
Restructuring the entire prompt system into modular, intent-specific prompts. Auditing all production prompts, mapping interdependencies, and rebuilding for continuous improvement.
Quality Evaluation
Building the feedback loop that connects monitoring to action — surfacing failure modes, creating prioritised fix lists, and validating every change before it reaches customers.
Conversational Commerce
Detecting buying intent in support conversations and routing toward conversion. Measuring chatbot-driven revenue in collaboration with IKEA's CRO team.
Voice AI
Contributing to Billie's expansion into voice-based customer service — a new channel requiring different prompting strategies and tighter latency management.
Knowledge Architecture
Automated pipelines that detect when source content changes, flag stale documents, generate draft updates, and check for terminology drift across all 63 markets.
Intent Classification
Small, fast language models achieving over 80% classification accuracy at sub-400ms latency in early experiments — dramatically outperforming the existing system.
From Support to Sales

Turning every conversation into an opportunity

Customers routinely signal buying intent inside support conversations. Until now, Billie treated these as support queries and moved on. Valae is building a support-to-sales bridge that detects purchase signals, routes customers toward conversion, and measures chatbot-driven revenue — transforming Billie from a cost-reduction tool into a revenue engine.
